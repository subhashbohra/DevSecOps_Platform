## The compensation ask
Lead with value, not hardship. Pick the phrasing that feels natural:

- *"Given the trajectory of my contributions and where the market sits for this work, my pay hasn't moved in two years — that's now significantly out of step with the value I'm delivering, and I'd like to talk about correcting it."*
- *(Optional, one light line only):* *"Honestly, the flat compensation is also becoming hard to sustain."* → then go straight back to impact.

**Then ask for a specific next step:**
- *"What would it take to correct this — an off-cycle adjustment given the PIP-to-award turnaround, or a defined number and plan for the next cycle?"*
- *"Can I count on you to advocate for this?"*

---

## Guardrails
- ✅ Lead with results; let the numbers land before money comes up
- ✅ Own the PIP story as a strength
- ✅ Walk in with a target number / band in mind
- ❌ Don't apologize or hedge ("I know I was on a PIP, but…")
- ❌ Don't compare yourself to named colleagues
- ❌ Don't dwell on the financial-need angle — one mention max
