# 16 — Referral Strategy

> A referral roughly multiplies your odds of a recruiter screen and signals quality. At your level, a *warm intro to the right team/manager* matters even more than a generic referral. Cold applications are your last resort, not your first move.

---

## The referral hierarchy (best → worst)

1. **Warm intro to the hiring manager / team** by someone who's worked with you. Gold.
2. **Referral from a current senior employee** on or near the target team.
3. **Referral from any current employee** (still strongly beats cold).
4. **Recruiter inbound** (you've built a profile worth approaching — your Community Builder/public presence helps here).
5. **Cold application** with a strong, level-appropriate resume. Last resort.

> Your goal: never apply cold to a target company. Spend the networking effort up front.

## The 5-step referral playbook

1. **Map the company.** Find 3–5 people you have *any* connection to (ex-colleagues, AWS CB peers, alumni, LinkedIn 2nd-degree). Note their team and seniority.
2. **Reconnect before asking.** If it's been a while, re-establish genuinely — comment on their work, a short catch-up. Don't lead with the ask cold.
3. **Make the ask easy.** Send a specific role link, a 2-line "why me," and your resume. The easier you make it, the more likely they say yes.
4. **Target the team, not just the company.** Ask if they can intro you to the hiring manager or someone on the team — far stronger than a portal referral.
5. **Follow up and close the loop.** Thank them regardless of outcome; update them; offer reciprocity. Referrers remember how you treat them.

## Outreach templates

> Also saved in `templates/`. Personalize every one — generic asks get ignored.

**A. Reconnect (warm-but-dormant contact)**
> Hi [Name] — it's been a while! I've been following [their work/post/team] — [specific genuine comment]. I'm exploring senior platform/infra roles and [Company] is high on my list. Would you have 15 minutes to catch up this week? No agenda beyond reconnecting — and I'd value your read on the team there.

**B. Direct referral ask (closer contact)**
> Hi [Name] — hope you're well. I'm applying for [Role + link] at [Company] at the [level] level. Given my work on [1-line: e.g., an air-gapped AI RCA platform that got bank-wide architecture-board approval, and a 50-pipeline lakehouse migration], I think it's a strong fit. Would you be open to referring me, or introducing me to the hiring manager? I've attached my resume and can send anything that makes it easy. Thanks either way!

**C. AWS Community Builder peer**
> Hi [Name] — fellow AWS Community Builder here. I'm looking at [Role] at AWS/[Company]. I've been building in public (acloudresume.com, an AWS Weekly newsletter, [talk]) and going deep on [platform/AI infra]. Would you be open to a quick chat about the team / a referral if it fits?

**D. Recruiter response (when they reach out)**
> Thanks for reaching out, [Name] — I'm open to the right opportunity. Quick context: I lead platform engineering at a global bank (lakehouse on GKE/GDC; air-gapped AI RCA platform approved bank-wide). I'm targeting [level] roles. Could you share the level, team, and comp band so I can gauge fit? Happy to set up a call.

**E. Cold application follow-up (when no referral exists)**
> Hi [Name/recruiter] — I applied for [Role] (req [#]) and wanted to add brief context: [2-line scope-narrative]. I'd welcome the chance to discuss how this maps to the role. Resume attached.
