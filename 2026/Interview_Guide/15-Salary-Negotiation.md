# 15 — Salary Negotiation

> The highest-ROI hour of your whole search. Negotiation is information + leverage + composure — not aggression. Read this *before* the recruiter screen, not after the offer.

> ⚠️ All figures are indicative for India and change constantly. Verify everything on **levels.fyi** and against your own offer. This is general guidance, not financial advice — model your own numbers and consider a financial advisor for large equity decisions.

---

## Universal principles (apply to every company)

1. **Negotiate level first.** Comp follows level. Pushing L5→L6 (Google) or Senior→Principal moves the band far more than haggling base. Make the level case during the loop (`01`'s scope narrative), not after.
2. **Never give the first number.** "I'm focused on finding the right fit; I'm sure you'll make a competitive offer. What's the range for this level?" Anchor on *their* range and market data.
3. **Get competing offers / signals.** Leverage is real alternatives. Run loops in parallel so offers land close together. Even a strong pipeline elsewhere is leverage.
4. **Total comp, not base.** Compare base + equity (and its vesting curve + likely appreciation) + sign-on + bonus. The shape differs wildly by company.
5. **Everything is negotiable, politely.** Base, equity, sign-on, start date, level, refresh expectations. Ask for the whole package; let them choose what to move.
6. **Stay warm and collaborative.** Recruiters are not adversaries; they often advocate for you internally. Be the candidate they want to fight for.
7. **Get it in writing; don't accept on the call.** "Thank you — can you send that in writing? I'll review and come back by [date]." Always take time.
8. **Know your walk-away** before you start. Decide your minimum acceptable total comp now (`15`'s offer sheet).

## Google (L5/L6)

- **Structure:** base + annual RSUs (typically front-loaded vesting at recent grants) + target bonus.
- **Levers:** **level (L5 vs L6 is everything)**, equity, sign-on. Base has bands; equity has the most room.
- **Tactic:** Use the **recruiter + comp committee** structure — the recruiter packages your case to the committee, so *give them ammunition* (competing offers, your level evidence). Push the L6 case hard if your cross-team impact supports it.
- **Refreshers matter:** ask about refresh/equity philosophy — initial grant isn't the whole story.

## Atlassian (Principal)

- **Structure:** base + RSUs + bonus.
- **Levers:** level (Principal band), equity, base. Values-aligned, collaborative process — negotiate in good faith, no hardball theatrics.
- **Tactic:** Lean on Principal-scope evidence and any competing principal-level offers. Equity is meaningful; clarify vesting and refresh.

## Pure Storage (Principal)

- **Structure:** base + RSUs + bonus; equity-heavy.
- **Levers:** level, equity, base. Public company — RSU value is more concrete than a startup's.
- **Tactic:** Depth-of-expertise candidates have leverage; tie your storage/lakehouse depth to the level. Ask about refresh and the equity's recent performance.

## AWS (Principal SA, L7)

- **Structure (distinctive — understand it):** modest **base** (there's a regional base cap), large **RSU** grant with a **back-loaded** vesting curve (historically ~5/15/40/40 over 4 years), plus **Year-1 and Year-2 sign-on bonuses** to fill the early gap while RSUs are light.
- **Levers:** level (L6 vs L7), **sign-on bonuses** (very negotiable — they exist precisely to bridge), total target comp (TTC), and equity. Because base is capped, you negotiate TTC and sign-ons, not base.
- **Tactic:** Ask the recruiter for the TTC target at your level and the sign-on structure. Model the *4-year average* and the *cliff in Year 3+* (when sign-ons stop and you depend on RSU vesting + refreshers). Negotiate sign-ons to smooth Years 1–2.

## Deloitte (Director / Senior Director)

- **Structure:** mostly **cash** (base + bonus), limited/no equity; partner track is a longer game.
- **Levers:** base, bonus target, level (Director vs Senior Director), title. Joining-bonus possible.
- **Tactic:** Total cash comparison vs the tech offers; the shape is different (predictable cash, less upside). Negotiate base + bonus + level; clarify the path/timeline to Senior Director/Partner.

## Scripts

**When asked for current/expected comp (deflect):**
> "I'd rather not anchor on a number early — I'm optimizing for the right role and level. I trust you'll put together a competitive package for this level. Could you share the band you're working within?"

**On receiving an offer (always buy time):**
> "Thank you, I'm genuinely excited. Could you send the full breakdown in writing? I'd like to review it properly and come back to you by [2–3 business days]."

**The ask (collaborative, specific):**
> "I'm really keen to join. Based on the level and the competing conversation I'm having, I was hoping we could get the total comp closer to [X], primarily through [equity / sign-on]. Is there flexibility there?"

**Closing with a deadline you control:**
> "If we can get to [X], I'm ready to sign and stop my other processes. Can we make that work?"

---

## Mistakes to avoid
- Giving a number first / revealing your floor.
- Negotiating base when the company's lever is equity/sign-on (AWS).
- Accepting on the phone in the excitement.
- Comparing only base, ignoring vesting shape and equity risk.
- Burning warmth with hardball — recruiters advocate for likeable candidates.
- Forgetting to negotiate **level**, the biggest lever of all.
