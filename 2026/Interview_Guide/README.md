# interview-playbook-2026

> A personal, principal-track interview preparation system for **Subhash Bohra** — DevOps Architect, Platform Engineer, AWS Community Builder — targeting **Google L5/L6, Atlassian Principal, Pure Storage Principal, NVIDIA Senior/Principal, AWS Principal SA, and Deloitte Director** roles.
>
> Target compensation band: **₹1 Cr – ₹1.5 Cr+ total compensation.**

This repository is a working system, not a reading list. Everything here is designed to be *checked off, tracked, and updated weekly*. Treat it like infrastructure: version it, commit your progress, and review the trackers every Sunday.

---

## Who this is for

You already have 20+ years of experience, an Engineering Excellence Award, a CAB-approved AI platform, and deep Kubernetes/GCP/AWS scars. You are **not** starting from zero. The risk for senior engineers is *not* lack of knowledge — it is:

1. **Rusty coding fundamentals.** You architect, you don't grind LeetCode daily. Interviews still test it.
2. **Underselling scope.** You do principal-level work but describe it like a senior IC.
3. **Story sprawl.** You have 20 years of stories and ramble. Interviewers score crisp, quantified STAR.
4. **Calibration mismatch.** L5 vs L6, Senior vs Principal — the bar is about *scope and influence*, not lines of code.

This playbook is built to fix exactly those four gaps.

---

## How to use this repository

1. **Week 0 — Calibrate.** Read `01-Career-Strategy.md` and `14-Leadership-Principles.md`. Decide your *primary* and *backup* targets. Do not prep for all six companies equally.
2. **Daily — Execute.** Follow `19-90-Day-Execution-Plan.md`. 2 hours weekdays, 4 hours weekends. Log it in `18-Weekly-Tracker.md`.
3. **Coding — Pattern first.** Use `02-DSA-Roadmap.md`. Learn the *pattern*, then the problems. Tick the checkboxes.
4. **System design — Reps.** Work through `03-System-Design.md`, `10-AWS-System-Design.md`, `11-GCP-System-Design.md`. One design per weekend, out loud, on a whiteboard.
5. **Behavioral — Bank your stories.** Fill in `12-STAR-Stories.md` once, polish them, reuse everywhere.
6. **Company-specific — Just before loop.** Read the relevant `04`–`08` guide 1–2 weeks before each company's loop.
7. **Mocks — Non-negotiable.** Schedule mocks from week 4. Log every one in `17-Mock-Interview-Tracker.md`.
8. **Negotiate — Don't wing it.** Read `15-Salary-Negotiation.md` *before* any recruiter screen, not after the offer.

> **Rule of thumb:** if a file has a checkbox, you are expected to tick it. If it has a tracker, you are expected to fill it. The value of this repo is in the discipline, not the prose.

## The three milestones

### 🟢 30-Day Milestone — "Foundations rebuilt"

By end of Day 30 you should have:

- [ ] Completed the **14-day DSA pattern sprint** (all 15 patterns reviewed)
- [ ] Solved the **60 MUST KNOW** problems at least once
- [ ] Written and polished **all 9 STAR stories** in `12-STAR-Stories.md`
- [ ] Completed **3 system design walkthroughs** out loud
- [ ] Locked your **primary + backup target companies**
- [ ] Sent **first 5 referral / networking messages**
- [ ] Done **1 coding mock** and **1 behavioral mock**

### 🟡 60-Day Milestone — "Interview-capable"

By end of Day 60 you should have:

- [ ] Re-solved MUST KNOW problems + completed **40 GOOD TO KNOW**
- [ ] Completed **8 full system designs** (incl. AWS & GCP specific)
- [ ] Done **6 mocks total** (2 coding, 2 system design, 2 behavioral)
- [ ] Refined STAR stories based on mock feedback
- [ ] Have **at least 2 active referral pipelines**
- [ ] Read company guides for primary + backup targets
- [ ] Can articulate your **L6/Principal scope narrative** in 90 seconds

### 🔴 90-Day Milestone — "Interview-ready"

By end of Day 90 you should have:

- [ ] All MUST + GOOD KNOW problems solid; attempted **10+ STRETCH**
- [ ] **11 system designs** done; can lead any one cold
- [ ] **10+ mocks** logged with consistent "hire/strong hire" signal
- [ ] Negotiation strategy rehearsed; comp targets and walk-away set
- [ ] Active loops scheduled or completed with ≥2 target companies
- [ ] A repeatable weekly maintenance routine (so skills don't decay)

## Operating principles

- **Depth over breadth at this level.** A Principal who can go three layers deep on tradeoffs beats one who name-drops ten technologies.
- **Quantify everything.** "Reduced migration effort" → "reduced a 50-pipeline migration from N person-months to a config-driven framework solved by one engineer."
- **Lead with influence.** At L6/Principal, the question is *who else did your work change*. Bank-wide adoption, CAB approval, award — these are your differentiators. Use them.
- **Stay calm under ambiguity.** Senior loops deliberately under-specify. Clarify, state assumptions, drive.
