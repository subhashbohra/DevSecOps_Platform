# 05 — Atlassian Interview Guide (Principal)

> Atlassian is **values-driven and practical**. The coding is more "build a working thing well" than abstract puzzle, and the **Values interview** is a real gate — strong technical candidates get rejected on values mismatch. For Principal, system design + technical leadership dominate.

> ⚠️ Comp figures are indicative for India; verify on levels.fyi and with your recruiter.

---

## Hiring process

1. **Recruiter screen** — fit, level, motivation, values primer.
2. **Initial technical screen** — coding (often practical/applied) or a take-home-style problem.
3. **Onsite loop** — typically **4–5 rounds**:
   - 1–2 **coding** (applied, clean-code focused)
   - 1–2 **system design / architecture** (heavy for Principal)
   - 1 **Values interview** (the famous one — *always* present)
   - 1 **leadership / cross-team influence** (for Principal)
4. **Debrief & decision** — panel aligns; values feedback weighted heavily.
5. **Team match + offer.**

---

## Atlassian's values (memorize — they *will* test these)

- **Open company, no bullshit** — transparency, candor.
- **Build with heart and balance** — craft + sustainability.
- **Don't #@!% the customer** — customer obsession.
- **Play, as a team** — collaboration over individual heroics.
- **Be the change you seek** — initiative, continuous improvement.

> The Values interview asks for *real stories* mapped to these. Pre-map your STAR stories (`12`) to each value.

---

## Coding expectations

- More **applied and realistic** than Google: parse/transform data, build a small feature, extend messy code. Tests your *engineering*, not just algorithms.
- They value: clean, readable, **tested** code; incremental development; sensible naming; handling edge cases without prompting.
- For Principal, raw algorithm difficulty is lower, but they expect *exemplary* code quality and judgment.

---

## System design expectations (the core for Principal)

- Design real systems with realistic constraints; deep tradeoff discussion.
- Principal signal: technical strategy, multi-team architecture, evolving a system over time, migration paths, operability.
- Expect questions adjacent to Atlassian's domain: collaboration tools, large-scale multi-tenant SaaS, eventing, search, permissions at scale.

---

## Behavioral / leadership expectations

- Heavy on **influence without authority**, mentoring, driving alignment, conflict resolution.
- Values interview: candor, customer focus, teamwork, initiative — with specific stories.
- Principal: "how have you shaped technical direction beyond your own team?"

---

## Questions interviewers commonly ask

- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your team's direction — what did you do?" (Open company / candor)
- "Describe a time you put the customer first even when it was hard." (Don't #@!% the customer)
- "When did you choose the sustainable solution over the fast one — or vice versa?" (Heart and balance)
- "How have you influenced architecture across multiple teams?" (Principal)
- "Walk me through a system you'd redesign today and why."

## Questions you should ask

- "How does the Principal role here set technical direction vs the architecture function?"
- "How do teams balance autonomy with platform consistency?"
- "What does 'no bullshit' look like in practice when there's disagreement?"
- "What are the biggest scaling/tech-debt challenges this org faces?"

## Compensation (indicative, India, verify live)

- **Principal Engineer:** total comp roughly **₹1.1 Cr – ₹1.8 Cr+** (base + RSUs + bonus), equity meaningful.
- Atlassian offers RSUs; negotiate level and equity (`15`).

---

## Preparation roadmap (6–8 weeks)

- **Weeks 1–3:** Coding with a *clean-code + testing* emphasis (MUST-60, but practice writing production-quality, well-named, tested solutions, not just AC).
- **Weeks 2–5:** System design reps, SaaS/multi-tenant flavor; 2 design mocks.
- **Weeks 3–6:** Map every STAR story (`12`) to the 5 values; rehearse the Values interview specifically.
- **Weeks 5–8:** Leadership/influence stories for Principal; full mocks.

---

## Common rejection reasons

| Reason | Fix |
|--------|-----|
| Values mismatch / generic value stories | Pre-map real stories to each value; be candid & customer-focused |
| Sloppy or untested code | Practice clean, tested, incremental coding |
| Thin Principal-scope evidence | Lead with cross-team influence (CAB/bank-wide adoption) |
| Treating it like a pure-algorithm shop | Show engineering judgment, not just optimal Big-O |
